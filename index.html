Getting There.

Getting to Nice

If you're flying to Nice you'll almost certainly arrive at Nice Cote d'Azur International Airport. Getting into the city is quick, simple, and cheap.

Bus (Recommended):

The 98 bus goes from the airport right along the promenade des anglais (waterfront). Costs around 6 euro, leaves about every 20 min.

Taxis:

Work the way taxis usually do.

Getting to Villefranche-sur-Mer.

Cécile is at pains to point out that our wedding is not in Nice. It is in Villefranche-sur-Mer, which is about 10 minutes from the center of Nice by car (or Taxi). There's also a train station which is a 5 minute walk from the wedding venue, with arrivals/departures every half hour, but this stops running at 22:30.

If you hire a car you could drive, but parking can be tight. We recommend getting the train on the way there, and getting a taxi back - it's not far, and there will also be lots of nice co-wedding-guests you can share with :-)

A place to stay.

Villefrance-sur-Mer is a beautiful place - a seaside village in itself. But Nice is also as its name suggests, and almost as convenient. The waterfront spots in Villefranche are usually booked out long in advance, and if you stay further up the hill you're a bit further from the buzz. We'd suggest staying in Nice - there are tons of places centrally located. Especially if you arrive earlier in the week, Nice has the most options for exploring. But they're (basically) the same place, so just find somewhere that suits you.

Wherever you decide to stay, booking.com and airbnb seem to have a great range of options.
